# Hardware Security Conferences

This page lists conferences, events, and meetups focused on hardware security, with detailed information to help you plan your participation and professional development.

## Why Attend Hardware Security Conferences

Hardware security conferences offer unique benefits for researchers, professionals, and enthusiasts:

- **Knowledge exchange** - Learn about cutting-edge research and emerging attack vectors
- **Hands-on workshops** - Develop practical skills through guided workshops and training
- **Networking** - Connect with leading experts and potential collaborators/employers
- **Tools and techniques** - Discover new tools, methodologies, and approaches
- **Research presentation** - Share your own findings with the community

## Conference Selection Guide

### By Career Stage

| Career Stage | Recommended Conferences | Benefits |
|---|---|---|
| **Students** | DEF CON, CCC, University workshops | Affordable, hands-on learning, networking |
| **Early Career** | DEF CON, BSides events, Regional conferences | Skill building, community connections |
| **Mid-Career** | Black Hat, REcon, Hardwear.io | Professional networking, specialized training |
| **Researchers** | CHES, USENIX, IEEE S&P | Publication opportunities, research connections |
| **Executives** | RSA Conference, Black Hat Executive Summit | Industry trends, strategic insights |

### By Interest Area

| Interest | Top Conferences | Notable Features |
|---|---|---|
| **IoT Security** | IoT Village (DEF CON), IoTSF Conference | Device hacking, firmware analysis |
| **Automotive** | escar, Car Hacking Village (DEF CON) | Vehicle systems, CAN bus, telematics |
| **Industrial Systems** | S4, ICS Village (DEF CON) | SCADA, PLCs, industrial protocols |
| **Mobile Hardware** | Mobile Security Summit, REcon | Baseband, secure elements, TEEs |
| **Cryptographic Hardware** | CHES, Real World Crypto | Side-channel analysis, secure implementations |

## Planning for Conferences

### Before the Conference
- **Research talks and workshops** - Plan your schedule to maximize value
- **Prepare equipment** - Bring appropriate gear for the villages you plan to visit
- **Set goals** - Determine what you want to learn or who you want to meet
- **Join community channels** - Follow conference Twitter/Discord for updates

### During the Conference
- **Participate in villages** - Get hands-on experience with hardware
- **Take notes** - Document key learnings and contacts
- **Network strategically** - Connect with speakers and other attendees
- **Balance sessions and hands-on** - Mix talks with practical activities

### After the Conference
- **Review materials** - Most conferences post slides and recordings afterward
- **Follow up with contacts** - Connect on LinkedIn or other platforms
- **Share learnings** - Consider writing a blog post or giving a talk at your local meetup
- **Apply knowledge** - Implement what you've learned in your work
